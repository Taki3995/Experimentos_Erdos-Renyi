Introduction: Grow and preferential attachment
In real networks the number of nodes grows continually thanks to the addition of
new nodes. For example: the publication of new research papers. In order to
model these networks, we need models of network growth.
Nodes prefer to link to the more linked nodes. For example: more citations on a
paper leads to more reads on it and it is more likely to be cited.

Introduction: Grow and preferential attachment
The random network model differs from real networks in two important
characteristics:
Growth: the ER model assumes that the number of nodes is fixed
(time invariant) while real networks are result of a continuous growing
process.
Preferential attachment: in random networks nodes randomly
choose their interaction partner randomly while in real networks nodes
prefer to link to the more linked nodes.

Degree dynamics
The BA model offers a dynamical description of a network’s evolution
capturing the fact that in real networks nodes arrive one after the other.
During the network’s evolution nodes compete for links where the older
nodes have an advantage over the younger ones, eventually turning into
hubs.
